# Mastering Diverse Domains through World Models

Authors: Google
Expended reivew: Yes
category: Dreamer3
conference: Nature
link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2301.04104
published date: 2023년 1월 10일

DreamerV3는 기존에 있던 Dreamer 계열 world model을 모델의 추가 적응 없이 여러 환경에 적응할 수 있도록 일반화를 시켰다.

Author introduce DreamerV3, a general algorithm that performs in 150 diverse tasks, such as ‘Minecraft’ with single configuration.

Dreamer learn environment model and improves its behavior by imagining future scenarios.

In this process, robustness techniques based on normalization, balancing, and transformations enable stable learning across domains.

## Introductions

Previous specialized algorithms (e.g. PPO) in reinforcement learning (RL) require substantial effort, expertise and computational resource in applying to new task such as video game moving and robotics. Thus, authors present Dreamer, a general algorithm that outperforms specialized expert algorithms across a wide range of domains while using fixed hyperparameters, making RL readily applicable to new problems.

And previous world models require scaling the representation loss differently based on the visual complexity of the environment.

Learn to ‘collect diamonds in Minecraft’ requires AI to solve sparse rewards, exploration difficulty, long time horizons and the procedural diversity of this open world game. Due to these obstacles, previous approaches resorted to using human expert data and domain-specific curricula. Authors achieve this challenge with DreamerV3 which did not use these scheme.

## Methods

Dreamer consists the spirit of three neural networks.

1. World model predicts the outcomes of potential actions

2. The critic judges the value of each outcomes

3. The actor choose actions to reach the most valuable outcomes.

The components are trained concurrently from replayed experience while the agent interacts with the environments.

## World Model Learning

### What world model doing?

**Flow of RL based on environment**

$$
x_t → a_t → r_t, x_{t+1}
$$

- $$x_t$$: environment from real world or encoder(optional) in time $$t$$.
- $$a_t$$: action in time $$t$$ from agent model $$\pi(a \mid x)$$.
- $$r_t$$: reward of agent’s action $$a_t$$ in time $$t$$. $$r_t = R(x_t, a_t, x_{t+1})$$

In non-world model scheme, given $$x_t$$(data) from environment, agent model predicts best action $$a_t$$ and gets reward $$r_t$$ and next environment $$x_{t+1}$$. There is no understand of environment, but just refined representation $$x$$ from environment.

**Flow of RL based on world model**

The world model understands highly-compact representation of domain(world) through auto-encoding and enables planning by predicting future representations and rewards for potential actions.

<img src="/assets/images/Dreamer3/figure2.png" class="img-medium" alt="Figure 2">

Given data $$(h_t, a_t, z_t)$$ where $$h_t$$ is encoder’s output of $$x_t$$, world model predict belows to understand environment(world).

- $$h_{t+1}$$: Next hidden state from sequence model (GRU/LSTM)
- $$z_{t+1}$$: latent representation of environment from Encoder (CNN-based VAE)
- $$\tilde{z}_{t+1}$$: Predicted latent representation of environment from Dynamics Predictor (MLP)
- $$\tilde{c}_{t+1}$$: Predicted continue state of environment from Continue predictor (MLP)
- $$\tilde{r}_{t+1}$$: Predicted reward from Reward predictor (MLP)
- $$\tilde{x}_{t+1}$$: Predicted environment from decoder (CNN) for training encoder and representation quality

Purpose of world model is simulating dynamics of target environment. So, quality of agent is not consideration in training world models.

The concatenation of $$h_t$$ and $$z_t$$ forms the model state from which predicted rewards $$r_t$$ and episode contunuation flags(게임이 끝났는지) and reconstruct the inputs to ensure informative representations.

In this process, the encoder and decoder use CNN for image input and MLP for vector input. And the representations are sampled from a vector of softmax distributions which can take straight-through gradients through the sampling step.

**RSSM**

Authors show Dreamer as a Recurrent State-Space Model (RSSM). It flows following steps. 

1. An Encoder maps sensory input $$x_t$$ to stochastic representations $$z_t$$.
2. A Sequence model with recurrent state $$h_t$$ predicts the sequence of these representations given actions $$a_{t-1}$$.

**The roll of Decoder**

The decoder forces the latent state to retain enough information to reconstruct the original observations. This prevents the encoder from keeping only reward-relevant features and discarding environment details. Thus, the world model learns richer and more informative representations for accurate dynamics and planning.

### Calculate Loss

Given a sequence batch of inputs $$x_{1:T}$$, actions $$a_{1:T}$$, rewards $$r_{1:T}$$, and continuation flags $$c_{1:T}$$, the world model parameters $$\phi$$ are optimized end-to-end to minimize the prediction loss $$\mathcal L_{\mathrm{pred}}$$, the dynamic loss $$\mathcal L_{\mathrm{dyn}}$$, and the representation loss $$\mathcal L_{\mathrm{rep}}$$ with weighted sum(where  
$$\beta$$ is weights).

$$
\mathcal L(\phi) = \mathrm E_{q\phi} \Big [ \ \sum^T_{t=1}(\beta_{\mathrm{pred}} \mathcal L_{\mathrm{pred}}(\phi) + \beta_{\mathrm{dyn}} \mathcal L_{\mathrm{dyn}}(\phi) + \beta_{\mathrm{rep}} \mathcal L_{\mathrm{rep}}(\phi)) \ \Big ]
$$

**Prediction loss**

$$
L_{\text{pred}}(\phi) = - \ln p_\phi(x_t \mid z_t, h_t)  -\ln p_\phi(r_t \mid z_t, h_t) - \ln p_\phi(c_t \mid z_t, h_t)
$$

The prediction loss trains the decoder and reward predictor via the **symlog squared** loss and continue predictor via logistic regression.

**Dynamics loss**

$$
  L_{\text{dyn}}(\phi)
  = \max\!\left(
  1,\;
  \mathrm{KL}\big[
  \operatorname{sg}(q_\phi(z_t \mid h_t, x_t))
  \,\|\,
  p_\phi(z_t \mid h_t)
  \big]
  \right)
$$

The dynamics loss trains the sequence model to predict the next representation by minimizing the KL divergence between the predictor $$p_{\phi}(Z_t \mid h_t)$$ and the next stochastic representation $$q_{\phi} (z_t \mid h_t, x_t)$$ with free bit and updating only dynamic predictor.

**Representation loss**

$$
L_{\text{rep}}(\phi)
= \max\!\left(
1,\;
\mathrm{KL}\big(
q_\phi(z_t \mid h_t, x_t)
\,\|\,
\operatorname{sg}(p_\phi(z_t \mid h_t))
\big)
\right)
$$

The representation loss trains the representation to become more predictable allowing us to use a factorized dynamics predictor for fast sampling. ($sg$ means ‘stop gradient’) By doing this, we can align two different representations from encoder and Dynamic predictor with only updating encoder.

즉, predict loss는 decoder와 reward predictor에 연결되어 둘을 학습시키고, Dynamic loss는 dynamic predictor의 latent와 관측 데이터의 임베딩된 latent 사이의 분포를 KL-divergence를 통해 낮추게 된다. 마지막으로, Representation loss는 Latent representation의 복잡도를 줄이고 구조화된 상태 공간으로 만듬으로써 world model의 빠른 샘플링을 돕는다.

이 과정에서 흥미로운 것은 같은 loss 항에 gradient 흐름만 끊음으로써 model collapse를 막을 수 있다는 것인데, 만약 encoder와 dynamic loss가 같은 loss 함수를 공유한다면, 두 모델은 서로의 분포에 무의미하게 가까워지게 된다.

### **Dilemma of Regularizing in various domains**

In dynamic terms, Complex 3D environment contain unnecessary details for control, thus it require strong regularization. On the other hand, the case with static background domain, it require weak regularization. Between this dilemma, author suggest **free bits** and **small representation loss** to solve dilemma.

**Free bit**

Free bit는 KL-divergence에서 posterior $$q(z \mid x)$$와 $$p(z)$$가 지나치게 가까워지는 것을 방지함으로써 latent에 담기는 정보의 압축 정도를 조절할 수 있다.

$$
\mathrm{Loss} = \mathrm{reconstruction \ loss} + \beta \cdot KL
$$

$$KL$$이 줄어들수록, $$x$$로부터 생성되는 분포들의 유사도가 올라기 때문에 latent에 담기는 정보의 다양성이 줄어들게 된다. 따라서 Free bit 방법에서는 $$KL$$ 대신 $$KL^{\prime} = \mathrm{max}(\lambda, KL)$$을 사용하여 $$KL$$의 하한선을 정해준다.

**Small representation loss**

representation loss는 dynamic model 분포와 encoder 분포 간의 정렬 정도를 강제하는 loss이다. 이 항을 상대적으로 작게 가져감으로써 encoder 분포는 dynamic predictor의 분포의 영향을 덜 받게 되며, 이로 인해 오로지 관측 정보를 embedding하는데 집중할 수 있다. (관측 능력은 reconstruction loss의 영향만 받음)

## Critic & Actor Learning

Two different architecture get input as $$s_t = (h_t, z_t)$$. At the code, two models composed with MLP(not official).

### Critic Learning

> The actor and critic neural networks learn behaviors purely from abstract trajectories of representations predicted by the world model.
> 

The actor and critic operate on model states $$s_t = \{ h_t, z_t \}$$ so take advantage of Markovian representations from recurrent world model. And basically, actor and critic model are each defined like below.

$$
\mathrm{Actor}: \ \ a_t \sim \pi_{\theta}(a_t \mid s_t) \ \ \ \ \ \mathrm{Critic}: \ \ v_{\psi}(R_t \mid s_t)
$$

The actor aims to maximize the return $$R_t \doteq \sum^{\infty}_{\tau=0} \gamma^{\tau}r_{t+r}$$ with a discount factor $$\gamma = 0.997$$ for each model state. To estimate returns that consider reward beyond the prediction in circumstance of impossible to see infinite future, they compute boostrapped $$\lambda$$-return that integrate the predicted rewards and the values with $$R^{\lambda}_t \doteq r_t + \gamma c_t \big( (1-\lambda)v_t + \lambda R^{\lambda}_{t+1} \big)$$.

In here, critic learns to predict the distribution of the future’s total reward value $$v_t \doteq \mathrm{E}[v_{\psi}(\cdot \mid s_t)]$$.

For simply put, reward predictor only learn reward of single step from $$z_t$$, but critic model learn distirubtion of entire rewards of sequence.

월드 모델이 통해 제한된 길이의 ($$T=16$$)미래 state 및 acting의 궤적을 상상하면, $$s_t$$로부터 $$v_T$$(무한 미래의 전체 보상의 기댓값)를 예측하도록 학습된 critic model은 더 먼 미래 환경을 고려한 행동에 대한 reward value의 분포를 예측한 뒤 해당 분포의 평균을 reward value로 출력한다. 따라서 $$\lambda$$-boostrap은 어디까지 월드 모델의 reward를 참고하고 어디서부터 critic model의 보상 추측 값을 사용할 것인지를 나타낸다.

### Actor Learning

> The actor learns to choose actions that maximize return while exploring through an entropy regularizer.
> 

** What is entropy regularizer? ** 
    
Entropy is uncertainty of policy $$\pi(a \mid s)$$. Decrease entropy is mainly related with confidence of model’s prediction, but at the same time, over decrease of entropy can cause overconfidence of prediction like overfitting.

$$
\mathcal H(\pi(\cdot \mid s)) = - \sum_a \pi(a \mid s) \log \pi (a \mid s)
$$

Find a balance between uncertainty and over confidence, most of policy model add entropy regularizer $$\alpha \mathcal H (\pi)$$ in cost function.
    

## Robust predictions

> Reconstructing inputs and predicting rewards and returns can be challenging because the scale of these quantities can vary across domains. We suggest the symlog squared error as a simple solution to this dilemma.
> 

**What is symlog(symettric log scale)?**

<img src="/assets/images/Dreamer3/figure4.png" class="img-medium" alt="Figure 4">

$$
f(x) =\begin{cases}\operatorname{sign}(x)\,\log\left(1 + \frac{|x|}{C}\right) & \text{if } |x| > C \\x & \text{if } |x| \le C\end{cases}
$$

즉, ‘0 근처는 거의 선형, 절댓값이 큰 수치들은 로그 스케일로 압축 + 음수까지 표현’이 가능한 함수이다.

**What is symlog squared loss?** 

Author suggest symlog squared error as a simple solution.

$$
L(\theta) \doteq \frac{1}{2}(f(x, \theta) - \mathrm{symlog}(y))^2
$$

$$
\hat{y} \doteq \mathrm{symexp}(f(x, \theta))
$$

A neural netwrok $$f(x, \theta)$$ with inputs $$x$$ and parameter $$\theta$$, learns to predict a transformed version $$\hat{y}$$ with inverse function of symlog (a.k.a symexp).

이러한 symlog error loss function은 단순 squared error 함수보다 안정적이다. 다양한 도메인에서 같은 함수를 사용하다보면 타겟 $$y$$의 값의 스케일이 변함에 따라 loss variance가 매우 커지는 반면, symlog 공간에서 학습을 하면 loss variance가 줄어들기 때문에 안정적인 gradient flow가 가능해진다.

Furthermore, they use symlog two-hot loss for potentially stochastic target. 

**what is two-hot?**
    
For example given bin $$[0, 1, 2, 3, 4]$$ and predicted value is $$2.3$$,

- one-hot representation: $$[0,0,1,0,0]$$
- tow-hot representation: $$[0,0,0.7,0.3,0]$$

Finally loss term become..

$$
\mathcal{L}(\theta) \doteq - \mathrm{twohot}(y)^T \log \mathrm{softmax}(f(x, \theta))
$$
